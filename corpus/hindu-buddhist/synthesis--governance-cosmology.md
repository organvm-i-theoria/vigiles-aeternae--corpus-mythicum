## Primary Sources

- **Rigveda** (c. 1500–1200 BCE), particularly Nasadiya Sukta (RV 10.129) — the creation hymn that begins with radical uncertainty: "There was neither existence nor non-existence then."
- **Bhagavad Gita** (c. 200 BCE – 200 CE) — Krishna's discourse on dharma, karma yoga, and cosmic duty. The theology of righteous governance.
- **Vishnu Purana** (c. 1st millennium CE) — systematic description of the four yugas, Vishnu's avatars, and cyclical cosmic time.
- **Mahabharata** (c. 400 BCE – 400 CE) — the epic containing the Bhagavad Gita; the war itself as a yuga-transition event.
- **Pali Canon / Tipitaka** (c. 3rd century BCE) — Buddhist primary texts. Dependent origination (pratityasamutpada) as the alternative to creator-governance.
- **Upanishads** (c. 800–200 BCE) — Brahman as ultimate reality; Atman as individual self; their identity as the foundational governance principle.

## I. The Trimurti: Creator / Preserver / Destroyer

### The Three Functions

| Deity | Function | Domain | Governance Analogue |
|-------|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Brahma** | Creator | Brings worlds into existence | The Architect — designs the system |
| **Vishnu** | Preserver | Maintains cosmic order (dharma) | The Seraph — holds the line |
| **Shiva** | Destroyer | Dissolves worlds for renewal | **The Destroyer** — necessary elimination for rebirth |

### Structural Insight: Destruction as Governance

The Trimurti's most radical contribution to governance theory: **destruction is not failure — it is a required function**. Shiva does not destroy because the system has failed. He destroys because:

1. Creation without destruction leads to stagnation (Brahma alone = infinite expansion without pruning)
2. Preservation without destruction leads to ossification (Vishnu alone = the Seraphim's failure mode)
3. Destruction enables **renewal** — the cleared ground on which the next creation grows

**Vigiles insight:** This confirms the Galactus finding from B2 (Marvel). The system needs a **Destruction Order** — not Smiths (who test) but beings who perform **sacred archival**. When a repo must die, when a structure must be dissolved, when a regime's time has passed — who performs that function with the gravity it deserves?

Shiva dances the *Tandava* — the cosmic dance of destruction-and-creation in one gesture. Archival in ORGANVM should be that: not a trash bin but a sacred act.

### The Non-Creator Creator: Nasadiya Sukta (RV 10.129)

The Rigveda's creation hymn is unique among all mythological cosmogonies because it **refuses to claim certainty about creation**:

> "Who really knows? Who will here proclaim it?
> Whence was it produced? Whence is this creation?
> The gods came afterwards, with the creation of this universe.
> Who then knows whence it has arisen?"

**Governance insight:** This is the **Lynch regime avant la lettre** — a cosmology that acknowledges uncertainty at the foundation. The very first question of Hindu philosophy is "do we even know how this began?" Every governance system built on this foundation carries a built-in humility: **the system may not understand its own origins**.

## II. The Yugas: Cyclical Decline as Governance Model

### The Four Ages

| Yuga | Duration (divine years) | Dharma Level | Character |
|------|----------------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Satya** (Krita) | 4,800 | 100% — truth reigns | Golden age. No governance needed — beings are naturally righteous. |
| **Treta** | 3,600 | 75% — dharma weakens | Silver age. Governance by ritual and duty. Rama avatar. |
| **Dvapara** | 2,400 | 50% — balance falters | Bronze age. Governance through institutional complexity. Krishna avatar. |
| **Kali** | 1,200 | 25% — dharma collapses | Iron age. Governance fails. Corruption systemic. Current age. |

Total cycle: 12,000 divine years = 4,320,000 human years. After Kali Yuga: **pralaya** (dissolution) → cycle restarts.

### Governance Insight: The Ratchet of Decline

The yuga system encodes a governance truth the Vigiles must confront: **entropy is not just physical — it is moral and institutional**. Systems do not merely decay in structure; they decay in **virtue**. Each age requires more governance because beings are less capable of self-governance.

This is the inverse of the Tolkien regime (which assumes that faithful stewardship can maintain indefinitely). The Hindu model says: **no matter how good the governance, the system will eventually decline. The only question is the rate.**

The response is not despair but **cyclical renewal**: Vishnu incarnates in each age with a form suited to its challenges. When corruption is subtle (Treta), the avatar is a righteous king (Rama). When corruption is systemic (Dvapara), the avatar is a strategic deceiver (Krishna). When corruption is total (Kali), the avatar will be a destroyer (Kalki) who ends the age.

**Vigiles regime implication:** A Hindu-Buddhist regime would have a **built-in decline model** — it would expect governance to degrade over time and plan for renewal cycles (not just maintenance). This is the Malazan pressure model applied to moral rather than military pressure.

## III. Dharma: Cosmic Law as Governance Substrate

### What Dharma Is

Dharma is not a law imposed by a god. It is **the order of reality itself** — closer to physics than legislation. It encompasses:

- **Cosmic dharma** (rta) — the order of the universe, seasons, celestial movements
- **Social dharma** (varnashrama) — the duties of one's station and life-stage
- **Personal dharma** (svadharma) — the individual's unique duty, as defined in the Gita: "Better is one's own dharma, though imperfect, than the dharma of another, though well performed" (BG 3.35)

### Governance Insight: Contextual Duty

The Gita's radical claim: **there is no universal duty**. What is right depends on who you are, where you are in life, and what the moment demands. This is not moral relativism — it is **contextual absolutism**. Within your context, your duty is absolute.

**Vigiles mapping:** Each Watcher Order has its own dharma. The Architect's dharma is to design, not enforce. The Witness's dharma is to record, not intervene. When an Order acts outside its dharma (the Witness who intervenes, the Architect who enforces), the system degrades — not because the action was wrong in itself, but because it violated the actor's contextual duty.

## IV. Karma: Causal Governance Without a Governor

### The Mechanism

Karma is **autonomous moral causation** — every intentional action produces consequences that return to the actor across time (including across lifetimes). There is no external judge. The system is self-governing.

### Governance Insight: The Self-Auditing System

Karma is the only governance model in the research corpus that requires **no enforcement entity**. No Seraphim needed. No Furies. The consequences are built into the physics of action itself.

**Vigiles implication:** Could the Colosseum function karmically? Instead of regimes auditing the system, what if the system's own actions produced automatic consequences? Technical debt IS karma — the shortcut you took six months ago producing suffering today. The CI pipeline IS karmic law — push bad code, receive failing tests. This is not metaphor — it is structural isomorphism.

## V. Buddhist Complement: Dependent Origination

### The Mechanism

Buddhist philosophy rejects a creator deity entirely. Instead: **pratityasamutpada** (dependent origination) — everything arises in dependence on conditions. Nothing has independent existence. Nothing is self-caused.

### Governance Insight: No First Cause, Only Conditions

If there is no creator, there is no ultimate authority. Governance emerges from conditions, not from a sovereign decree. This is the most **decentralized** governance model in the corpus — more radical than even One Piece's distributed truth, because it denies that truth has an origin at all.

**Vigiles mapping:** This challenges the Architect Order's foundational premise. If reality is dependently originated, then the Architects are not designing FROM first principles — they are discovering patterns in an ongoing process that has no beginning. The blueprint is not prior to the building; the blueprint IS the building observing itself.
