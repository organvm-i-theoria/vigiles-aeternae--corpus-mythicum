## Primary Sources

- **Popol Vuh** (K'iche' Maya, transcribed c. 1554-58 from older oral tradition) — Creation myth, Hero Twins, Xibalba underworld. The most studied indigenous document of Mesoamerica.
- **Codex Borgia** (pre-Columbian, c. 1200-1521) — Ritual calendar and cosmological manuscript. Tonalpohualli (260-day sacred calendar) as governance structure.
- **Florentine Codex** (Bernardino de Sahagún, c. 1545-90) — 12-volume ethnographic encyclopaedia of Aztec life. Primary source for Fifth Sun myth and sacrifice practices.
- **Leyenda de los Soles** (Legend of the Suns, c. 1558) — Aztec cosmogonic narrative. The five creations and destructions.
- **Sun Stone** (Aztec Calendar Stone, c. 1502-21) — Monumental sculpture encoding the Five Suns cosmology.

## I. The Five Suns: Serial Creation-Destruction

### The Cycle

The Aztec cosmos has been created and destroyed **four times**. We live in the Fifth Sun. Each prior creation ended in catastrophe:

| Sun | Presiding God | Destruction | Duration | Governance Lesson |
|-----|-------------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| 1st (Jaguar) | Tezcatlipoca | Jaguars devoured humanity | 676 years | Power without wisdom consumes its subjects |
| 2nd (Wind) | Quetzalcoatl | Hurricane destroyed all | 364 years | Knowledge without stability scatters |
| 3rd (Rain/Fire) | Tlaloc | Rain of fire | 312 years | Fertility without restraint burns |
| 4th (Water) | Chalchiuhtlicue | Great flood | 676 years | Emotion without structure drowns |
| **5th (Movement)** | **Tonatiuh** | **Earthquake (prophesied)** | **Current** | **Balance requires constant sacrifice** |

### Governance Insight: Serial Prototype Failure

Each Sun is a **governance prototype that failed**. The gods tried different approaches — brute power, knowledge, fertility, emotion — and each produced a world that couldn't sustain itself. The Fifth Sun is the **fifth attempt**, built from the lessons of four failures.

**Vigiles mapping:** This is the most explicit prototype-and-iterate model in the corpus. The cosmic system fails, the governors analyze the failure, they try again with modifications. This is TDD at cosmic scale — test, fail, learn, rebuild. ORGANVM's own promotion pipeline (LOCAL → CANDIDATE → PUBLIC_PROCESS → GRADUATED) is a compressed version of the Five Suns: each stage is a prototype that must survive its test or be destroyed.

## II. Sacrifice as Cosmic Fuel: The Metabolic Governance Model

### The Mechanism

The Fifth Sun exists through **reciprocal sacrifice**. The gods sacrificed themselves to create it — Nanahuatzin threw himself into the fire to become the sun. But the sun would not move without more blood. Only through regular human sacrifice — hearts offered as "precious eagle-cactus fruit" — could the sun continue its journey.

This is NOT arbitrary cruelty. It is a **metabolic model of governance**: the system consumes energy (sacrifice) to maintain order (the sun's movement). Stop the sacrifice → the sun stops → the Fifth Sun ends in earthquake → total destruction.

### Governance Insight: Governance Costs Resources

Every other tradition in the corpus describes governance maintenance in abstract terms (ritual, knowledge, balance). Mesoamerica makes the cost **literal and visceral**: governance consumes lives. Maintaining the system requires taking from the system. The governed must fuel their own governance.

**Vigiles mapping:** This is the most honest statement of a universal truth: **governance has a non-zero cost**. Running CI consumes compute. Running audits consumes time. Running the Agon consumes attention and context. Every regime that governs extracts something from the system it governs. The only question is whether the extraction is acknowledged (Mesoamerica) or hidden (most other traditions).

The Mesoamerican model forces the question: **is the cost of governance worth the governance?** If the sacrifice exceeds the value of what's preserved, the system is parasitic on itself.

## III. The Hero Twins: Governance Through Adversarial Play

### The Popol Vuh Narrative

In the Popol Vuh, the Hero Twins (Hunahpu and Xbalanque) descend to Xibalba (the underworld) to confront the Lords of Death. They succeed not through force but through **play** — specifically the Mesoamerican ballgame. They trick the Lords, die, are reborn, and ultimately defeat death through cleverness, sacrifice, and refusal to accept the rules as given.

### Governance Insight: The Ballgame as Governance Contest

The Mesoamerican ballgame (*pitz* in Maya, *ullamaliztli* in Nahuatl) was simultaneously:
- A **sport** (athletic competition)
- A **ritual** (cosmic reenactment of the Hero Twins' victory)
- A **governance mechanism** (disputes between cities settled by ballgame)
- A **sacrifice platform** (losers — or sometimes winners — were sacrificed)

This is the **Colosseum made literal**. The Agon as a physical game where competing philosophies play out through embodied contest, and the stakes are real.

**Vigiles mapping:** The ballgame IS the Colosseum. Competing regimes enter the court. They play according to rules. The outcome determines governance. The cost is real (sacrifice of the loser — or in the Vigiles, the rotation of the failing regime). The Hero Twins' strategy — understanding the rules well enough to subvert them — is the Smith Order's function.

## IV. Quetzalcoatl and Tezcatlipoca: The Eternal Duality

### The Two Poles

| Deity | Domain | Method | Color |
|-------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Quetzalcoatl** (Feathered Serpent) | Wind, knowledge, life, the morning star | Opposes fatal sacrifice, favors auto-sacrifice (blood-letting from own body) | White |
| **Tezcatlipoca** (Smoking Mirror) | Night, conflict, sorcery, the jaguar | Demands sacrifice, rules through fear and power | Black |

Their eternal conflict drives the Five Suns cycle. Each takes turns ruling. Each destroys the other's creation. The Fifth Sun is the **unstable equilibrium** between them.

### Governance Insight: Governance as Perpetual Agon

The Quetzalcoatl-Tezcatlipoca duality IS the Agon at the cosmic level. Two irreconcilable governance philosophies — knowledge-based mercy vs. power-based extraction — taking turns, each destroying the other's world, each learning from the destruction, each creating the next attempt.

**Vigiles mapping:** This is the Tolkien-Malazan polarity expressed as divine war. Quetzalcoatl = Tolkien (knowledge, restraint, mercy). Tezcatlipoca = Malazan (power, survival, extraction). The Fifth Sun is the regime that tries to hold both.
